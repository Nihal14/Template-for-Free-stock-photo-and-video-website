What are Copy-Right-Free Images ?

A copyright-free image is provided by a photographer or owner, and can be used by any person. The photographer or owner owns the copyright but provides the legal authority that the image can be freely used by others. You can usually find copyright-free images on public domains or copyright-free image libraries.


Copyright-free images are images that can be used without having to obtain permission or pay royalties to the owner of the copyright. These images are either in the public domain or have been released by the copyright owner under a Creative Commons license. Public domain images are those that are not protected by copyright, either because the copyright has expired, the owner has relinquished their rights, or they were created by the government. Public domain images can be used for any purpose, without restriction. Creative Commons licenses are a way for creators to share their work while retaining some control over how it is used. There are several different types of Creative Commons licenses, but most allow for non-commercial use of the image, as long as credit is given to the creator.
